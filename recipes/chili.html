Confetti Chili

Description

I call this confetti chili because it's flecked with multicolored vegetables: golden corn, red peppers, orange carrots, and black and red beans. Together they add excitement and texture to the beefy flavor-packed base, while chipotle chili in adobo gives it a spicy, smoky kick.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil, extra virgin
  • 1 tablespoon small onion, diced (1 cup)
  • 1 tablespoon medium red bell pepper, seeded and diced (1 cup)
  • 1 tablespoon medium carrot, diced (1/2 cup)
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1/2 tablespoon chili powder
  • 2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 pound lean or extra-lean (90% lean or higher) ground beef
  • One 28-ounce can crushed San Marzano Tomatoes, with their juices
  • 2 cup water
  • 1 can ned chipotle chile in adobo sauce, seeded and minced, plus 2 teaspoons of the sauce
  • 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
  • One 15.5-ounce can black beans, only partially drained
  • One 15.5-ounce can pinto beans, only partially drained
  • 1 1/2 cup frozen corn kernels
  • Salt and fresly ground black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Heat the oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion, garlic, bell pepper, carrot, and chili powder. Cover and cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, about 10 minutes.
  3. Add the cumin and coriander and cook, stirring, for 1 minute.
  4. Add the ground beef; raise the heat to high and cook, breaking up the meat with a spoon, until the meat is no longer pink.
  5. Stir in the tomatoes, water, chipotle and adobo sauce, and oregano and bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce the heat to medium-low and cook, partially covered, stirring from time to time, for 30 minutes.
  7. Stir in the beans with some of thier juices. Continue cooking, partially covered, 20 minutes longer, until the chili is nicely thickened.
  8. Stir in the corn and cook until heated through.
  9. Season with salt and pepper and serve.
